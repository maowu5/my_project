Journey to the West

The story revolves around the quest of the monk Xuanzang,

who is tasked with retrieving Buddhist scriptures from India to bring them back to China.

On his journey, he is protected by three magical disciples: Sun Wukong,

a mischievous and powerful monkey with extraordinary abilities; Zhu Bajie,

a gluttonous pig-like creature with a good heart but lazy tendencies; and Sha Wujing,

a loyal and quiet river ogre. Along the way, they encounter various demons, monsters,

and challenges, many of whom wish to eat Xuanzang to gain immortality. Through a series of trials,

they overcome these dangers, all while learning lessons about virtue, loyalty, and perseverance.

Author

Wu Cheng'en (c. 1500–1582) was a Chinese novelist and poet during the Ming Dynasty. He is best known as the author of Journey to the West, one of China's Four Great Classical Novels. His work is celebrated for blending mythology, folklore, and allegory with a humorous and satirical style, creating a story that has had a lasting impact on Chinese literature and culture. Though Wu Cheng'en lived a relatively quiet life, his literary legacy endures through his imaginative storytelling and vivid characters.
